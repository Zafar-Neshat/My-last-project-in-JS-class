About us

History of TM Institute was established in 2006 to train the new generation of Afghan artisans and was registered in March 2011 as a non-governmental and non-profit legal entity separate from Firouzkouh Institute in the government of the Islamic Republic of Afghanistan. This was done to promote more participation of Afghans, handing over the institute to Afghans was important for the survival and improvement of the project. This institute educates students in the fields of calligraphy and painting, traditional wood works, ceramics and jewelry making, and the students are trained by some of the most experienced Afghan masters and are among the best historical art works of Afghanistan in their fields. They get inspiration. In addition to practical classes, students learn beliefs, interpretation of the Great Quran, Islamic culture, business science, Dari and Pashto literature, English, information technology, geometric design, drawing, graphic design, technical drawing, technical mathematics and art history. In 2009, the first group of students graduated from the three-year program of this institute.

Services

TM Institute

The purpose of Firouzkoh Afghan Institute of Arts and Architecture Education was to train a new generation of professionals in the field of industries and arts, to revive and restore endangered traditions, to fulfill the talents in Afghanistan and to meet the needs of expanding markets inside and outside the country. . This institute has quickly become a leading institution in the field of arts and industries education in the country and has taken a special place among the arts and industries centers of the country. Training of 122 (96 males and 26 females) students in three-year programs of this institute in traditional works on wood, ceramics, calligraphy and miniatures, jewelry and jewelry making and 57 (29 males and 28 females) in one-year programs in Carpentry, jewelry making and sewing departments. Providing support to graduates: 225 students (151 male and 74 female) who graduated from Firoz Koh institute, 94% of them are provided with employment in the relevant profession or continue their studies in higher education institutions (domestic and foreign) has been In 2013, Firouzkoh Institute exhibited more than 46 works of art prepared by the professors and students of this institute at the exhibition of the Museum of Islamic Arts held in Doha, the capital city of Qatar.
